⚙️ How the AI Works

1
Training Data: The AI is seeded with music theory rules — scale intervals, common chord progressions (I–IV–V–I, ii–V–I), and rhythmic patterns typical of the chosen mood.
2
Markov Chain Model: A 2nd-order Markov chain learns transition probabilities: given the last 2 notes, what note is most likely next? This mirrors how LSTM models learn note sequences.
3
Music Theory Constraints: Notes are constrained to the chosen scale. The AI applies phrase shaping (rising tension, falling resolution) and avoids large dissonant leaps.
4
Generation: Starting from a seed note, the model auto-regressively predicts each next note — identical to how neural text/music generators work.
5
Synthesis: Notes are played using the Web Audio API (OscillatorNode + ADSR envelope) and visualized on the live piano roll — equivalent to MIDI playback.
